Welcome!

I am a graduate student at the University of Florida pursuing a PhD in experimental high energy particle physics. My research involves searching for new physics using data collected by the Compact Muon Solenoid at the Large Hadron Collider. My analysis utilizes machine learning methods to search for particles that may provide evidence for theoretical extensions to the scalar sector (think Higgs boson) of the standard model of particle physics. I am currently developing a set of neural network algorithms to tackle some of the most difficult challenges in these searches, including identifying signal events amongst a multitude of background events and sorting final states with high combinatorics.

Along with searching for new physics, I also contribute to the design and performance evaluation of a novel trigger algorithm, which will be integrated into future upgrades to the LHC. The novel trigger will be able to identify muons that originated from displaced vertices, which may lead to the detection of muons arising from new physics processes.
